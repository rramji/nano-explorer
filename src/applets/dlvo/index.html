Theory & Equations
Electrostatic double-layer (EDL) repulsion
VEDL = 64π ε₀εr R (kBT/ze)² γ² exp(−κh)
γ = tanh(zeψ₀/4kBT) · κ⁻¹ ≈ 0.304/(z√c) nm (water, 25 °C, c in mol/L)
van der Waals attraction (with retardation)
VvdW = −AR/(12h) · 1/(1 + 14h/λL), λL ≈ 100 nm
Derjaguin approximation valid for h ≪ R
Steric repulsion (de Gennes brush)
Vsteric = πR · (kBTL/s³) · [8/5·(2L/h)5/4 + 8/7·(h/2L)7/4 − 96/35] h ≤ 2L
s = grafting distance, L = brush height; V = 0 for h > 2L
Hydration repulsion
Vhyd = πR · C₀ · λ · exp(−h/λ)
Structured water layer at hydrophilic surfaces; λ ≈ 0.2–1 nm
Hydrophobic attraction
Vhp = −πR · C₀ · λ · exp(−h/λ)
Enhanced attraction between hydrophobic surfaces; λ ≈ 1–3 nm
Depletion attraction (Asakura–Oosawa)
Vdep = −Π · (π/3) · (rp−h/2)² · (6R + 4rp + h) h < 2rp
Π = npkBT (osmotic pressure); purely entropic, no surface forces
Magnetic dipole interaction
Vmag = −(μ₀/2π) · m² / (2R+h)³ (head-to-tail alignment)
m = Ms · (4π/3)R³; μ₀ = 4π × 10⁻⁷ H/m
Stability criterion
Vmax > 15 kBT → stable · 5–15 kBT → marginal · <5 kBT → rapid coagulation
Secondary minimum → reversible flocculation · Schulze–Hardy: CCC ∝ z⁻⁶
