Theory Reference

Derjaguin Approximation (Proximity Force Approximation)

For two curved surfaces at separation D ≪ R, the interaction force relates to the flat–flat free energy per unit area G(D) via:

F(D) = 2π Reff · G(D)

For non-retarded vdW, G(D) = −A/(12πD²), giving:

GeometryReffEDAValid when
Sphere–Sphere (equal R)R/2−AR / (12D)D ≪ R
Sphere–FlatR−AR / (6D)D ≪ R

Exact Hamaker Integration

Integrating pairwise −C₆/r⁶ over two body volumes yields closed-form results. For two equal spheres (s = 2R + D):

E = −(A/6) [ 2R²/(D(D+4R)) + 2R²/s² + ln( D(D+4R)/s² ) ]

For a sphere of radius R separated by D from a flat surface:

E = −(A/6) [ R/D + R/(D+2R) + ln( D/(D+2R) ) ]

The DA overestimates attraction at large D/R because it treats the distant parts of curved surfaces as locally flat — an increasingly poor assumption as D/R → 1.

Bead Model

Each sphere is packed with point atoms on a 3D Cartesian grid (spacing Δ ≈ atom diameter). Total vdW energy is the explicit pairwise double sum:

Enum = Σi∈obj1 Σj∈obj2 −C₆/rij⁶

C₆ is calibrated so that Enum matches the exact Hamaker result at a reference separation Dref = R. The sum converges toward the exact result as N → ∞ (Δ → 0). Reliability requires D ≳ Δ; below that, discretisation artefacts grow. Increasing N shrinks Δ and pushes that boundary to smaller separations.

Bead brightness encodes proximity to the opposite object — atoms nearest the gap glow hottest, showing where the interaction is concentrated.
