Theory & Equations
Keesom (orientation) interaction
VK = −C6K/r⁶ · C6K = μ₁²μ₂² / (3kBT)
Thermal average over all orientations of permanent dipoles. Requires μ ≠ 0. Decreases with T.
Debye (induction) interaction
VD = −C6D/r⁶ · C6D = μ₁²α₂ + μ₂²α₁
A permanent dipole polarises its neighbour. Requires at least one polar molecule. Temperature-independent.
London (dispersion) interaction
VL = −C6L/r⁶ · C6L = (3/2) α₁α₂ I₁I₂/(I₁+I₂)
Quantum-mechanical correlation between instantaneous dipole fluctuations. Present for all molecules. Dominant for nonpolar species.
Units & formulas
All formulas in CGS-Gaussian: μ in esu·cm (1 D = 10⁻¹⁸ esu·cm), α in cm³ (1 Å³ = 10⁻²⁴ cm³), I in erg (1 eV = 1.602×10⁻¹² erg). Convert C₆ to SI: × 10⁻¹⁹ → J·m⁶. V(r) = −C₆/r⁶ for each term.
Typical magnitudes (water–water, 25 °C)
C₆K ≈ 95 · C₆D ≈ 10 · C₆L ≈ 33 (all ×10⁻⁷⁹ J·m⁶)
For nonpolar molecules (e.g. Ar, CH₄), Keesom = Debye = 0; London dominates entirely.
Hamaker & macroscopic bodies
Summing pair-wise London interactions over two macroscopic half-spaces gives VvdW = −AHR/(12h) (Derjaguin), where the Hamaker constant AH ≈ π²ρ₁ρ₂C6L. See the DLVO applet for sphere–sphere interactions.
